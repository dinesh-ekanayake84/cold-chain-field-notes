# Cold-Chain & Inventory Management — Field Notes

> Practical observations and operational approaches from hands-on experience managing temperature-sensitive product logistics, storage facilities, and inventory control within promotional and event supply chains.

---

## Overview

This document captures real-world approaches to cold-chain handling and inventory management in a fast-paced outdoor media and promotions environment. The context involves managing food and beverage products distributed across multiple campaign sites — where maintaining product integrity, tracking expiry, and coordinating timely delivery are non-negotiable operational requirements.

These are field notes, not textbook theory. They reflect what works in practice.

---

## 1. Cold-Chain Handling

### The Core Challenge
Food and beverage promotional products have a fixed shelf life. In an event and campaign context, products move through multiple hands — warehouse staff, logistics teams, on-site activations crews — before reaching the end consumer. Every handoff is a risk point.

### Approach
- **Intake inspection** — Every inbound delivery is checked for temperature compliance, packaging integrity, and batch/expiry details before entering storage.
- **Segregated storage zones** — Temperature-sensitive items are stored separately from dry goods. Clear labelling and zoning prevents cross-storage errors.
- **FIFO discipline** — First In, First Out is applied consistently. Older stock is always rotated to the front of storage. This is simple in principle but requires active enforcement, especially during high-volume campaign periods.
- **Pre-dispatch checks** — Before any product leaves the warehouse for a campaign site, a condition and expiry check is completed. No product is dispatched without sign-off.
- **Transport coordination** — Delivery schedules are planned around temperature risk windows, particularly for extended transport routes or peak-heat periods.

## 2. Expiry Monitoring

### The Core Challenge
Managing expiry across multiple product lines, varying campaign durations, and fluctuating demand is one of the most operationally demanding aspects of promotional logistics. Waste is costly. Running out mid-campaign is worse.

### Approach
- **Expiry tracking log** — A running record of all stock, organised by product line, batch number, quantity, and expiry date. Updated at every intake and dispatch point.
- **Threshold alerts** — Products approaching expiry within a defined window are flagged for priority use or reallocation to upcoming campaigns.
- **Campaign demand forecasting** — Stock quantities are planned against projected campaign footfall and duration to minimise both overstock and shortfall.
- **Post-campaign reconciliation** — At the close of each campaign, remaining stock is counted, logged, and assessed. This informs future ordering decisions.

---

## 3. Storage Facility Management

### The Core Challenge
A promotional warehouse handles more than just products — it holds technical equipment, reusable display assets, printed materials, and consumables, all with different storage requirements and usage cycles.

### Approach
- **Category zoning** — Storage is divided into clear zones: cold storage, dry goods, equipment, reusable assets, and consumables. Each zone has its own access and management protocol.
- **Asset condition tracking** — Reusable items (display units, equipment) are logged in and out with condition notes. Damage is flagged immediately and actioned before the next deployment.
- **Stock replenishment cycles** — Minimum stock levels are set for key consumables. When stock falls below threshold, a replenishment order is triggered without waiting for a full stockout.
- **Space optimisation** — During high-campaign periods, storage space is managed actively. Inbound and outbound schedules are coordinated to prevent congestion and access delays.

---

## 4. Key Learnings

- **Documentation discipline matters more than systems.** A simple, consistently maintained log outperforms a complicated system used inconsistently.
- **Expiry management is a forecasting problem.** Most waste comes from poor demand planning, not negligence at the storage stage.
- **Cold-chain integrity is only as strong as the weakest handoff.** The warehouse can be perfect — but if the on-site team doesn't maintain conditions, the chain breaks.
- **Communication between logistics and campaign teams prevents most problems.** Late changes to campaign schedules, venues, or quantities are the primary source of operational disruption.
